# Worked Example — Incomplete Intake

**Engagement:** "Digital Transformation" for BuildRight Construction

---

## Problem Definition (Score: 25)

BuildRight wants to "digitally transform" their operations. They currently use spreadsheets for a lot of things and want to modernise. The MD thinks they need a better system.

**Why scoring 25:** Problem is mentioned but entirely vague. No current state detail. No quantified pain. No urgency stated. "Better system" is a solution direction, not a problem statement.

**Gap:** "Problem statement does not describe the current state in specific terms. What processes use spreadsheets? What does failure look like? What is the cost of the current state?"

---

## Expectations (Score: 25)

They want nterprise to come in and help. Flexible on approach.

**Why scoring 25:** No delivery format stated. No engagement model preference. No client involvement level. "Help" is not an expectation.

**Gap:** "Expectations not captured. What should nterprise deliver — analysis, software, implementation, advisory? What does 'help' mean operationally?"

---

## Success Criteria (Score: 0)

Not discussed during intake conversation.

**Why scoring 0:** Zero criteria captured. No basis for acceptance.

**Gap:** "No success criteria. Intake cannot proceed without at least 2 measurable, falsifiable criteria."

---

## Constraints (Score: 50)

Budget is flexible. They want it done "relatively quickly" — maybe by end of year. They're on Microsoft 365.

**Why scoring 50:** Technical constraint (M365) is present. Timeline is directionally stated but not a date. Budget is unspecified — "flexible" is not a constraint.

**Gap:** "Provide a budget range (even indicative). Convert 'end of year' to a specific date."

---

## Stakeholders (Score: 25)

Contact is John from BuildRight. He runs the IT team.

**Why scoring 25:** One contact named with a role. No economic buyer identified. No project sponsor. No information on who approves spend.

**Gap:** "Identify the economic buyer. Who approves the engagement commercially? Is John the decision-maker or a technical gatekeeper?"
