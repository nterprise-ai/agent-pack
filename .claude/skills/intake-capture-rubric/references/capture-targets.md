# Capture Targets

The following elements must be captured during intake to produce a scoreable record.

---

## 1. Problem Definition

**What it is:** A clear statement of the business problem the client is trying to solve — not the solution they think they want.

**Must capture:**
- The current state (what is happening now)
- The pain or cost of the current state
- Why solving this matters now (urgency or trigger)

**Acceptable:** "Our brokers spend 2 hours per week manually re-keying data between Guidewire and Salesforce. This causes errors and delays commission calculations."

**Not acceptable:** "We need an integration." (states a solution, not a problem)

---

## 2. Expectations

**What it is:** What the client expects nterprise to deliver and how they expect to work together.

**Must capture:**
- Delivery format (e.g. working software, analysis, report, prototype)
- Engagement model preference (fixed-scope, iterative, advisory)
- Involvement level expected from client side
- Any non-negotiable constraints on approach

**Acceptable:** "They want a working integration with documentation. Iterative delivery is fine. Their tech lead will be available 2 days/week. They do not want an offshore team."

**Not acceptable:** "They want it done properly." (too vague to score)

---

## 3. Success Criteria

**What it is:** The specific, verifiable outcomes that will tell both parties the engagement succeeded.

**Must capture:**
- At least 2 measurable success criteria
- Each criterion must be falsifiable (pass/fail at acceptance)

**Acceptable:**
- "Data syncs within 5 minutes of a policy update in Guidewire"
- "Zero manual re-keying errors reported in 30 days post-go-live"

**Not acceptable:** "Client is happy with the result." (not falsifiable)

---

## 4. Constraints

**What it is:** The limits within which the engagement must be delivered.

**Must capture at least 2 of:**
- Budget range (even indicative — e.g. "under $150k")
- Timeline (hard deadline or target date)
- Technical constraints (approved platforms, security requirements, on-prem vs. cloud)
- Resourcing constraints (nterprise headcount limits, client team availability)

**Acceptable:** "Budget is $80–120k AUD. Must go live before end of financial year (June 30). Must use their existing Azure tenant."

**Not acceptable:** Silence on all constraint categories.

---

## 5. Stakeholders

**What it is:** The people involved in making decisions and providing access.

**Must capture:**
- Economic buyer (who approves spend)
- Technical contact (who owns the systems)
- Project sponsor or champion (who is driving this internally)
- Any known blockers or opponents

**Acceptable:** "Sarah Chen (CTO) is the economic buyer. James Park (Integration Lead) is the technical contact. No internal blockers identified."

**Not acceptable:** Only a company name with no named contacts.
