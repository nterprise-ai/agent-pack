# Operational Fit Taxonomy

Signals used to classify an intake as strong, moderate, or weak operational fit for nterprise.

---

## Strong Fit

nterprise's highest-confidence delivery zone:

**Problem type:**
- AI-assisted workflow automation
- System integration (especially with well-known platforms)
- Data pipeline or analytics platform delivery
- Proposal or document intelligence

**Client characteristics:**
- Has a named technical contact with decision-making authority
- Understands they are buying implementation, not just advice
- Has approved or earmarked budget (not "we're exploring")
- Has a clear go-live target

**Engagement characteristics:**
- Defined scope (not open-ended)
- Iterative delivery acceptable
- Client team available for reviews and UAT
- Duration 4–16 weeks

**Examples:**
- "We need a working integration delivered, we have budget, and we have someone to own it"
- "We want a proposal pipeline that uses AI to draft documents"
- Any engagement matching the platform agent pattern exactly

---

## Moderate Fit

Deliverable within nterprise capabilities but requires more scoping care:

**Problem type:**
- Complex data strategy or architecture (heavy advisory component)
- Bespoke AI model development (not existing platform extension)
- Process re-engineering alongside technology delivery

**Client characteristics:**
- Budget not yet formally approved ("we expect to get sign-off")
- Technical contact exists but is junior or not empowered
- Multiple stakeholders with different views on scope
- Client has had a bad experience with prior vendors (requires trust-building)

**Engagement characteristics:**
- Scope likely to evolve during engagement
- Requires more governance overhead
- May have a discovery phase before implementation

**Examples:**
- "We want to use AI somewhere in our operations but aren't sure where"
- "We need a data strategy before we can decide on tooling"
- Advisory-heavy engagements where delivery is secondary

---

## Weak Fit

Outside nterprise's core delivery model — escalate or decline:

**Problem type:**
- Pure research or exploration with no defined deliverable
- Staff augmentation (body-shopping) without a project
- Managed services / ongoing support without an initial build component
- Large programme management without a technical delivery component

**Client characteristics:**
- No budget authority in the conversation
- "We're just getting quotes" with no timeline to decide
- Procurement process with 6+ vendors and unknown evaluation criteria
- Requires resources nterprise cannot supply (e.g. 20 offshore developers)

**Engagement characteristics:**
- Open-ended scope with no defined end state
- Primarily a body-of-knowledge transfer (training only)
- Requires nterprise to carry commercial risk (fixed-price delivery of undefined scope)

**Examples:**
- "We need 10 developers for 6 months to help our team"
- "Can you run our IT department for us?"
- "We want AI training for all staff" (no integration or delivery component)
